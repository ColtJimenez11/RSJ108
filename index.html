Colton Jimenez's How to Keep Your Journalism Classes Organized

An Instruction Challenge for JOUR108

How to stay organized ...

Assembly time: 55 minutes
Awesome rating: 100% organized awesomeness

Did going back to in person classes shake up your world a little bit? Are you wondering how you can start off next semester on the right foot? Below are instructions to help secure success in your organization for the following semester!

  • Things You'll Need

  • Phone, Tablet, Laptop or PC
  • Wifi connection or Other source to Internet
  • Google Account (Drive & Calendar)

  1. Add your class schedule to your calendar .

    Open google calendar and insert your class Day, Time, Location. Make sure that for each class you set them for recurring. You can Duplicate the classes over to other days as well. For example, If I had a Monday and Wednesday class at 2:30-3:45 I would create the class for Monday and duplicate it to Wednesday. Then I would set them to reoccur for 12 weeks (the length of the semester). .

  2. Create folders for each class

    Open Google Drive. Create a new folder and Label it “Spring/ Summer Year". Within the folder you just created, create 5 more folders (or however many classes you are in). Rename the new folders with the names of the classes you are enrolled in for the semester. Now you will have a designated and decluttered space for all of your electronic work, readings and assignments sheets.

    This is what your semester folder will look like once a few folders are added.

  3. Download the Syllabi

    Download each of your classes syllabi into each of the corresponding folders. Additionally download any Appendices or schedules that go with the class as well. This way when an assignment is assigned you’ll know exactly where to look for the instructions.

  4. Organize your Calendar

    Once you’ve downloaded the syllabi, on another tab open up your google calendar and enter in your assignments one by one. This may be a tedious task, to mitigate some time insert only your big assignments such as exams, presentations, projects and other

  5. Remain current on deadlines

    Continually check and update assignment dates throughout the semester. Although you put the assignments in your calendar for the correct days it’s not uncommon for due dates to change throughout the course of the semester.
